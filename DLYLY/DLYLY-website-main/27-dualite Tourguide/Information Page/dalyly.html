Did you know that the Great Pyramid of Giza, one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World, was originally covered in smooth, polished limestone casing stones? These casing stones reflected the sun's light, giving the pyramid a brilliant, gleaming appearance. Additionally, these casing stones were precisely cut and fitted together so seamlessly that it's said that not even a razor blade could fit between them. Over time, much of the casing stones were removed, but some still remain at the base of the pyramid, offering a glimpse into the pyramid's former grandeur and beauty.

Despite its name, Siwa Salt Lake isn't just an ordinary salt flat; it's actually an ancient saltwater lake that has been evaporating over thousands of years. What makes it truly fascinating is its unique composition. Unlike typical salt flats, Siwa Salt Lake is dotted with surreal, crystalline formations that resemble giant stepping stones, creating a mesmerizing and otherworldly landscape. These salt formations, sculpted by the evaporation of the lake's water, add to the lake's ethereal beauty and make it a captivating destination for photographers, adventurers, and nature enthusiasts alike.
